Threat Intelligence Tips

Learn how to identify and protect yourself from spam, phishing, and other digital threats with our expert advice.

Email Security

Spotting Phishing Emails

• Check the sender's email address carefully for misspellings
• Look for generic greetings like "Dear Customer"
• Hover over links to see the real URL before clicking
• Be wary of urgent requests for personal information
• Watch for poor grammar and spelling mistakes

SMS Security

Avoiding SMS Scams

• Never reply to messages asking for personal details
• Be suspicious of texts claiming you've won prizes
• Don't click on links from unknown senders
• Verify delivery notifications from services you didn't use
• Watch for messages pretending to be from banks or government

Social Media

Social Engineering Protection

• Be cautious of friend requests from strangers
• Don't share personal information in public posts
• Verify charity requests before donating
• Watch for fake profiles impersonating people you know
• Be skeptical of "too good to be true" offers

General Security

Strong Account Protection

• Use unique passwords for each account
• Enable two-factor authentication everywhere possible
• Regularly update your software and apps
• Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi networks
• Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity

Image Safety

Identifying Malicious Images

• Don't download images from untrusted sources
• Be wary of images with embedded QR codes
• Watch for images that ask you to "click to view"
• Check image file extensions before opening
• Use our image scanner for suspicious files

Financial Safety

Avoiding Financial Scams

• Banks will never ask for full passwords via email
• Verify investment opportunities with official sources
• Be cautious of "urgent" payment requests
• Check website security (HTTPS and padlock icon)
• Monitor accounts regularly for unauthorized transactions
